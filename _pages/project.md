Excavation Autonomy with Resilient Traversability and Handling
======
<video width="640" height="360" controls>
  <source src="/videos/Excavator-intro.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>


Excavation Autonomy with Resilient Traversability and Handling is a multi-year project focused on developing full-stack autonomous solutions for excavators. By leveraging advanced perception, planning, and control algorithms, the project aims to deliver reliable and safe autonomous systems capable of handling challenging tasks such as digging, lifting, and dumping. Additionally, these systems are designed to safely navigate complex and dynamic construction sites, ensuring both efficiency and resilience in demanding environments. (More : *[UB-NEWS](https://www.buffalo.edu/news/releases/2024/07/Moog-gift-engineering-autonomous-excavators.html)*, *[Project-Link](https://droneslab.github.io/EARTH/)*)

Intervention-Minimal Planning And Control on Off-Road Terrain
======
<video width="640" height="360" controls>
  <source src="/videos/impact_video.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>
Intervention-Minimal Planning and Control on Off-Road Terrain is a legged robot project focused on developing advanced navigation and mapping solutions for confined, rugged environments such as tunnels and caves. The project aims to create an autonomous solution capable of safely navigating through various terrains while generating 3D maps of environments. These capabilities are designed to support inspection duties and rescue missions in areas that are difficult or dangerous for human access.

Minimum-Time and Safe Planning and Control for F1TENTH Autonomous Racing
======
<video width="640" height="360" controls>
  <source src="/videos/f1tenth-demo.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>
F1TENTH Autonomous Racing is an annual international research competition dedicated to advancing the field of autonomous driving using 1/10-scale autonomous vehicles. This project aims to develop computationally efficient control and planning algorithms capable of real-time decision-making. The primary research objectives are to achieve time-optimal performance, ensure collision avoidance, and enable dynamic vehicle overtaking, in complex and competitive racing scenarios.
